How did this class change your view on Web Development?

Before taking DEV109 – Intro to Web Development, my view of web pages was that of ignorance.
Through taking this course and learning through its artifacts and modules, along with the help of the books and W3Schools, I was able to demystify my view on the field.
As I stated before three slides previously, I had no prior knowledge as to any of the framework of webpages, and so my understanding was greatly increased through simply understanding the basics behind HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
I’m now capable of pulling up the source code for a site and parsing through the bog of elements.

What would you recommend to future students taking this class?

Beyond simple programming advice, there’s a fair bit of general academic recommendations that students should heed.
It’s helpful to read through assignments as soon as they’re published, simply to have an understanding of the work needed to accomplish it. From there you can plan accordingly and allot yourself the necessary time.
Make sure you make proper use of the slack channel to get advice and tips from your peers. Perhaps you may even be able to help them.
If you don’t know how to do something, just Google it. Of course it’s better to get answers from the textbook, the instructor, or your peers, but for simple, one-line issues a simple search is all you need. There’s plenty of information online, especially on W3Schools.
